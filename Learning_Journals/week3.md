# Week 03: WordPress CMS & Group Interviews

## Learning Activities & Resources
*   Building my startup website either locally or online using the WordPress.org core software: [https://wordpress.org/](https://wordpress.org/).
*   Taking part in the class group formation activity, where we did quick one-minute interviews with each other in a round-robin style.
*   Thinking of good interview questions to ask my classmates about their working styles, availability, how hard they work, and what they expect from the project.
*   Writing down notes during the quick interviews to keep track of everyone's answers and help me choose my group members.

## Estimated hours of Explicit Learning Activity
6 hours

## Content Insights
*   When I set up WordPress, I saw big differences compared to Joomla, especially in how themes and widgets work. It made me realize why it is important to keep different projects in separate environments so they do not crash or mix up.
*   I found out that using a structured "job interview" style to talk to classmates is a really fast and good way to find the right team members. It works much better than just picking friends randomly.
*   Looking back at my interview notes, I noticed that everyone has very different ideas about how much time to spend on the project and when they are free. This showed me how important it is to talk about these expectations before forming my team.

## Career/Employability/Learning Insights
*   Doing the fast one-minute interviews felt like real-world networking or hiring. It helped me practice how to quickly understand what someone is good at and how they work.
*   This activity gave me good career insights about how teams are built in the real world. I realized that checking if people are a good fit early on can prevent a lot of problems and arguments later in the project.
*   I learned how to choose teammates based on clear facts like how reliable they are and when they can work, instead of just picking people I know. This is a very useful project management skill that I will definitely use when I work with others in the future.
